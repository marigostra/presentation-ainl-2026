\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Problem and Motivation}
\begin{itemize}
\item Morphological tagging = automatic assignment of grammatical features
\item Important for: MT, information extraction, audiobook stress positioning
\item Closed-vocabulary models fail on unseen words
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}


\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Morphological tagging}
\framesubtitle{  Why multi-head attention?}

\begin{exampleblock}{Open dictionary}
No methods that do not support an open dictionary were considered.
The~model must handle unknown words.
\end{exampleblock}

\begin{exampleblock}{No RNN}
No methods that include recurrent networks were considered.
RNN are noticeably outdated and have many disadvantages.
\end{exampleblock}

\begin{exampleblock}{BERT is too heavy}
BERT implies a~heavy training process, although it also contains multi-head attention inside.
\end{exampleblock}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Key Design Choises}
\begin{itemize}
\item BPE Tokenization → Open dictionary support
\item Optimal dictionary size: 2,198 tokens
\item Max 6 subwords per word (covers 98\% of cases)
\item Weighted aggregation (attention-based) → Preserves prefixes/suffixes
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}


\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Model and training}
\framesubtitle{  Technical details of~the~model and its training dataset}
\begin{block}{Model}
The~resulting model has the~size of 195MB and has around 48M weight parameters.
\end{block}
\begin{block}{Training}
For the training we used UD SynTagRus ver.~2.16 and UD Taiga ver.~2.17.
Merging them gave us nearly 190,000 training sentences.
The~training process takes approximately 10 hours on a single RTX4090 GPU and consist of 35 epochs.
As an optimizer we used AdamW.
\end{block}
\end{frame}
